Instrument

Risk taken: drops green as the brand color entirely — brand identity now rides a clinical blue.

Direction 02

Dossier

Every verdict is a case file. Keeps the warmth of the current design but moves the personality off the serif and onto the artifacts: rubber-stamp provenance, evidence pinned like index cards, a monospace field-notes voice. Memorable because it looks like a careful investigator’s desk — not an editorial magazine.

Risk taken: stamps/typewriter voice flirt with skeuomorphism — needs restraint to stay grown-up, not kitsch.

Direction 03

Wayfinding

Signage you trust on instinct — the visual grammar of transit and airport boards, repurposed for allergy decisions. Bold functional type, strict high-contrast panels, and color that always pairs with a word and an icon. The verdict is a departure-board panel: unmissable, calm through order rather than warmth.

Risk taken: high-contrast panels can feel loud/institutional — the discipline is keeping it quiet between verdicts.

Direction 04

Spectrum

One gesture organizes everything: a horizontal safe → caution → avoid axis. Restaurants aren’t a list of donuts, they’re plotted as positions along the spectrum, so “how do these compare?” is answered by the eye before you read a number. Minimal, airy, premium — the risk gradient is the only saturated thing on the page.

Risk taken: the axis metaphor must stay legible with many results and on mobile — the whole idea rides on that one component working.
